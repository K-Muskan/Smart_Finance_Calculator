ðŸ“š Method Explanation: Numerical Root-Finding

The Newton-Raphson and Secant methods find roots by iteratively improving guesses. For loan calculations, we solve for the payment amount M that satisfies the loan equation.

Newton-Raphson Method

xn+1 = xn - f(xn) / f'(xn)

Why chosen: Fast convergence (quadratic) when initial guess is close to the solution.

Limitations: Requires derivative; may fail if derivative is near zero.

Secant Method

xn+1 = xn - f(xn) Â· (xn - xn-1) / (f(xn) - f(xn-1))

Why chosen: No derivative needed; generally reliable convergence.

Limitations: Slightly slower than Newton-Raphson; needs two initial guesses.
